Construction Estimates
Whether you are a property owner or a contractor, you always want to have a clear and accurate forecast of how much a building project will cost and how long it will take to complete. However, the timeline for new building construction can have their fair share of snags along the way, and closing can be pushed back by a few days or in some cases few months. Variables such as permit hiccups, weather events, construction accidents, or labor shortages are all things that can draw out a construction timeline. Underestimating, as well as overestimating, can both have negative consequences which is why no matter what type of estimate you’re given, it’s important to remain flexible. Time and Labor
As we mentioned above, material costs can vary depending on the region, but labor costs can also vary depending on where your construction project is located as regional labor costs can play a role in determining costs. Geographic region and what workers are paid in that region is a key component of the cost per square foot of new building construction. Whether you need to hire an architect, carpenter, concrete professional, or a painter, each is paid differently depending on where you are at in the country.
UnExpected Suprises
When estimating building costs, common expenses are often overlooked. Building construction is a process and surprises are likely to come up along the way. Some of these surprises include:
  • Site preparation (clearing a lot of trees, hauling dirt, removing rocks)
  • Permit fees (local building codes, zoning laws, and restrictions)
  • Time of year (labor is in demand during times of unemployment and low economic growth which means costs are typically higher)
While the list of potential surprises will vary depending on the construction project, you should always make sure to have a bit of a financial cushion or emergency fund (we recommend at least 10%) so that you aren’t left without the money when a surprise does arise.
